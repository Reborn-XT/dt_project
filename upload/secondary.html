OBSERVATION

Registration & Appointment System

1

Manual Entry

Appointments are booked via call or walk-ins. Staff handwrites phone numbers, dates, and names in a physical register.

2

Data Disorganization

The register lacks proper organization. Missing dates and unclear starting points lead to confusion and potential data loss.

3

Helpful Staff

Two staff members successfully manage patient flow at the counter despite the disorganized manual process.

THE PROCESS

Accessible Fee

The appointment fee is highly accessible at just ₹200 for a consultation with the female dermatologist.

Patient History

Patients are required to maintain a physical file containing their previous visits, prescribed medicines, and doctor remarks.

Visual Tracking

Patients must take images of their skin issues at every visit to actively observe their progress until treatment is over.

CONSULTATION TIMELINE

Time and Recovery Expectations

⏳

Fast-Paced Visits

Each visit takes an average of 10 minutes, with only a 1-minute gap between consecutive patients.

📅

Monthly Prescriptions

Patients are typically provided instructions to take medicines and apply creams for an entire month.

✨

Recovery Speed

Results are usually visible within 2 weeks. In certain cases, full recovery is possible within just 3 visits.

MEDICATION DYNAMICS

Prescriptions & Costs

01. Dispensing Methods

Medicines are split: doctors directly provide some creams/liquids, while patients buy tablets, serums, gels, and facewashes outside.

02. Brand Tie-ups

Doctors often have tie-ups with big companies, prescribing specific branded medicines that are only available at a single designated shop.

03. Financial Burden

These branded medicines are extremely expensive, often costing upwards of ₹2000 per month, making it unaffordable for many Indian customers.

THE GENERIC GAP

Why Patients Overspend

Alternative Options

Generic medicines exist at a much lower price point, but they are incredibly hard to find without guidance.

Lack of Awareness

Patients only know brand names, not active components, making them fearful of side effects from alternatives.

Online Discrepancies

Even the prescribed branded medicines are sometimes cheaper online, but patients are not aware of this and continue to buy from the tied-up shop.

AFFINITY DIAGRAM

Categorizing Clinic Observations

Conclusion: Identify the Gaps

"While clinical care is efficient, data management and patient education are severely lacking."

Gap 1: Manual, disorganized registration risks data loss.

Gap 2: Expensive tied-up prescriptions create a financial burden.

Gap 3: Lack of awareness regarding cheaper generic alternatives.

Our Goal: Digitization & Education.

Creating a smarter, more affordable ecosystem for dermatology patients.
